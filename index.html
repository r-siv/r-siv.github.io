About Me

I am a Bioinformatics PhD student at UBC's Computational Biology Lab being co-supervised by Dr. Amrit Singh and Dr. Zachary Laksman.

My current research involves developing tools for disease biomarker discovery. Over the course of my PhD, I have worked on the development of multimodal deep learning models for predicting risk-associated biomarkers in patients affected by Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy. The aim of my research is to contribute to a deployable AI model that can improve patient risk stratification which will aid in treatment prioritization

Previously I interned as a research student at the Krembil Research Institute where I worked on identifying de-novo dispositions and pathways for patients affected by severe psoriatic arthritis

In my spare time, I enjoy exploring the outdoors and pursuing photography. You can view some of them here.
